About the Kingdom

According to several classical authors, the Colchis was an area which was surrounded by the Bridge, the Black Sea, the Corax river (probably the current Bzybi river, in Abkhazia, Georgia), the chain of the Greater Caucasus (included between the Colchis and the Sarmatian Kingdom), Iberia, the Moschici Mountains (today the Lesser Caucasus Mountains) and Armenia. However, the southern border of Colchis varies according to the classical authors: thus, Strabo starts the region at Trebizond, while Ptolemy, on the other hand, extends the Bridge to the Phase river.
We know today that Pitsounda was the last Colche city located in the north of the country.
It is in this region that the legends of Jason and the Argonauts are born, who went there to seek the Golden Fleece of the magician Medea. The name "Colchis" appears for the first time in the works of Aeschylus and Pindar.
The older writers referred to the region as Eea (the residence of the mythical king Etes and his sister Circe). As for Arrien, he draws up a list of Colches rivers which, according to him, were only simple mountain torrents: the Chariéis, the Chobus (or Cobus), the Singame, the Tarsarouras, the Hippus, the Astelephus and the Chrysorrhoas. The main cities of Colchis were Dioscourias (called Sevastopole by the Romans, today Sukhoumi), on the banks of Pont-Euxin, Sarapana (current Chorapan), Phasis (current Poti), Pityus (current Pitsounda), Apsaros (today Gonio), Surium (today Vani), Archéopolis (today Nokalakevi), Macheiresis and Cyta, also called Cutatisium (current Koutaisi), the traditional place of the birth of Medea.
